BeakBrain — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 July 2026

BeakBrain (“the app”, “we”) helps you learn to identify birds by sight and sound. This policy explains what we collect and how we use it. It is deliberately short because we collect very little.

Guest use — no data collected

You can use BeakBrain without an account. In that case everything you do — your progress, streak, saved trips and collections — stays on your device. Nothing is uploaded and we collect no personal information.

If you create an account

If you sign up, we store your email address and sync your learning progress, saved trips and collections so they follow you across your devices. That is the only personal data we hold. It is used solely to operate the app. We never sell or share it, and we show no ads and use no third-party advertising or tracking.

Accounts and data are stored with our backend provider, Supabase (PostgreSQL). Row-level security ensures only you can read your own data.

Deleting your data

You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time from Account → Delete account inside the app. This removes your data from your device and from our servers immediately. You may also email us to request deletion.

External links

The app links out to third-party sites (birding tours, bird-club directories, Wikipedia, Xeno-canto, BirdLife). Those sites have their own privacy policies; we are not responsible for them.

Children

BeakBrain is a general-audience educational app and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Media & data credits

Bird photos and recordings are used under open licences (CC0 / CC BY / CC BY-SA) from Wikimedia Commons, iNaturalist and the Xeno-canto community; taxonomy and occurrence data come from AviList and GBIF. Each item’s photographer/recordist and licence are shown in the app.
